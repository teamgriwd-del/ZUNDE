# Pigs — Zimbabwe Compliance & Health Reference

> Not legal or veterinary advice. See the [disclaimer](../README.md). For a real diagnosis, contact a CVSZ-registered vet.

## 1. Legal requirements to own, keep, and sell pigs in Zimbabwe

Pigs are legally classed as "stock" under the **Stock Theft Prevention Act [Chapter 9:18]**, meaning theft and receiving-stolen-pig offences carry the same criminal exposure as for cattle, sheep, and goats (see [stock-theft-act.md](../laws/stock-theft-act.md)). However, our research found that Zimbabwe's **branding/RFID identification infrastructure (dip tanks, brand certificates, national cattle ID programme) is built primarily around cattle**, with sheep and goats also covered by the historical Brands Act framework — **pigs were not confirmed in our research as subject to the same brand-certificate/dip-tank system.**

| Requirement | Why | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Proof of ownership** (purchase receipts, breeding records) | Pigs are covered by the Stock Theft Prevention Act; you should be able to show how you came to own each animal | [stock-theft-act.md](../laws/stock-theft-act.md) |
| **Disease reporting** for suspected notifiable disease (especially African Swine Fever) | Legal duty on the owner under the Animal Health Act framework | [animal-health-act.md](../laws/animal-health-act.md) |
| **DVS movement permit / veterinary clearance** for transport, especially during an ASF outbreak in the area | Pig diseases like ASF spread extremely fast; DVS can and does restrict pig movement in outbreak zones | [livestock-movement-permits.md](../laws/livestock-movement-permits.md) |
| **Biosecurity at point of sale** (buyer awareness of herd health status) | Not a specific pig-only statute found in our research, but a reasonable extension of the Animal Health Act's disease-control purpose and, for a Retailer, the Consumer Protection Act's disclosure duty | [animal-health-act.md](../laws/animal-health-act.md), [consumer-protection-retailers.md](../laws/consumer-protection-retailers.md) |

**We could not confirm from our research whether Zimbabwe requires a specific brand/tag/registration certificate for pigs the way it does for cattle.** Farmers/Suppliers listing pigs on PFUMA should still expect to provide ownership proof and disease-status disclosure, but should confirm with DVS whether any pig-specific identification scheme applies in their district.

## 2. Common diseases

| Disease | Key symptoms | Severity / notifiable status |
|---|---|---|
| **African Swine Fever (ASF)** | High fever, blue-purple cyanosis (discoloration) of snout, ears, tail, and lower legs, lethargy, loss of appetite, heavy discharge from eyes/nose; sudden death within 2–3 weeks of infection in many cases | Extremely severe — mortality can reach up to 100%; WOAH-notifiable in most countries; **no vaccine widely available** — control relies on biosecurity, culling, and movement restriction. Report suspected cases to DVS immediately |
| **Classical Swine Fever (CSF / hog cholera)** | Fever, huddling of sick animals, loss of appetite, dullness, weakness, conjunctivitis, constipation followed by diarrhoea, unsteady gait; later, purple discoloration of ears/abdomen/inner thighs | Highly infectious, often fatal, notifiable. **Clinically almost indistinguishable from ASF** — laboratory testing is required to tell them apart, so any suspicious die-off should be treated as a potential emergency regardless of which one it looks like |

## 3. Basic diagnosis guidance — when to call a vet vs. routine care

**Call a vet immediately (and expect this to trigger mandatory disease reporting) if you see:**
- Sudden, unexplained deaths in the herd, especially clustered over days.
- Skin discoloration (blue-purple/purple) on ears, snout, tail, legs, or abdomen.
- High fever combined with off-feed behaviour across multiple animals at once (suggests a spreading infectious cause rather than one sick individual).
- Any combination of the ASF/CSF symptoms above — because the two diseases look alike clinically, **do not attempt to self-diagnose which one it is**; get DVS/vet involvement to test and confirm.

**Routine care / monitor, but escalate if it worsens:**
- A single pig off its feed for less than a day with no fever and no herd-wide pattern.
- Minor skin scrapes/wounds from normal pen activity, cleaned and not showing spreading infection.

## 4. Vaccination / biosecurity schedule pointers

Our research did not surface a standardized, Zimbabwe-specific pig vaccination calendar with the same level of detail available for cattle, goats, and sheep (see [cattle.md](cattle.md), [goats.md](goats.md), [sheep.md](sheep.md)). General good-practice pointers for Zimbabwean smallholder-to-commercial pig keeping, to be confirmed with a vet/DVS for the current local programme:

- **Biosecurity is the primary defence against ASF** since no reliably available vaccine exists — control introduction of new pigs, disinfect visitor footwear/vehicles, avoid feeding untreated food waste/swill (a known ASF transmission route), and control contact with wild pigs/warthogs where relevant.
- **Routine deworming and standard piglet vaccinations** (e.g. against clostridial disease, erysipelas, where locally recommended) should follow a vet-set schedule adjusted to herd size and local disease pressure.
- **Report any suspected ASF/CSF case to DVS before moving, selling, or slaughtering any pig from the affected premises.**

## What we could not verify with confidence

- Whether pigs fall under any Zimbabwe-specific identification/branding scheme (beyond general Stock Theft Act ownership-proof obligations) — confirm with DVS.
- A confirmed, current, Zimbabwe-specific pig vaccination calendar — confirm with DVS or a livestock vet, as our sources covered cattle/goat/sheep/poultry but explicitly did not cover pigs in the vaccination-schedule source reviewed.
