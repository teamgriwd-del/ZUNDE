# Cattle — Zimbabwe Compliance & Health Reference

> Not legal or veterinary advice. See the [disclaimer](../README.md). For a real diagnosis, contact a CVSZ-registered vet.

## 1. Legal requirements to own, keep, and sell cattle in Zimbabwe

| Requirement | Why | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Brand registration** (brand certificate from civil registry, physical brand applied, typically left neck) | Legal proof of ownership; required for police clearance before a sale | [livestock-identification-act.md](../laws/livestock-identification-act.md) |
| **RFID ear tag** (national programme, progressive rollout) | Modern electronic traceability layer; cattle without a tag risk impoundment once rollout reaches an area | [livestock-identification-act.md](../laws/livestock-identification-act.md) |
| **Dip tank registration** | Ties the animal to a locality for tick control and area-based disease control/tracing | [livestock-movement-permits.md](../laws/livestock-movement-permits.md) |
| **Disease reporting** for suspected notifiable disease | Legal duty on the owner under the Animal Health (General) Regulations, 1994 | [animal-health-act.md](../laws/animal-health-act.md) |
| **DVS Veterinary Movement Permit** before transport/sale involving distance/district crossing | Confirms the animal is disease-free and fit to travel | [livestock-movement-permits.md](../laws/livestock-movement-permits.md) |
| **Police clearance** before/at point of sale | Confirms the animal isn't stolen, matches brand/tag to owner records | [stock-theft-act.md](../laws/stock-theft-act.md) |
| **Buyer/butcher record-keeping** (if reselling or slaughtering commercially) | Required under the Stock Theft Prevention Act for butchers/hide buyers | [stock-theft-act.md](../laws/stock-theft-act.md) |

A cattle listing on PFUMA that is missing brand/tag identification, or has no path to police clearance, should be treated as high-risk and flagged before a sale is facilitated.

## 2. Common diseases

| Disease | Key symptoms | Severity / notifiable status |
|---|---|---|
| **Foot and Mouth Disease (FMD)** | Fever, blisters/vesicles on nose, tongue, lips, mouth, between toes, above hooves, teats; excessive salivation; lameness | High transboundary/trade impact; WOAH-notifiable transboundary animal disease. Rarely fatal in adults, higher mortality in young calves |
| **January Disease (Theileriosis)** | Swelling of lymph nodes (under ears, shoulder), cloudy eyes, running eyes, rough coat, loss of appetite, difficulty breathing, froth from nose/mouth, collapse; often fatal, especially in older/weaker animals | Severe — tick-borne (brown ear tick), peaks Dec–Mar; has caused over 500,000 cattle deaths in Zimbabwe since 2016 per reporting reviewed. Report suspected cases to DVS |
| **Anthrax** | Sudden death often the first sign; also fever, staggering, difficulty breathing, bleeding from body openings; acquired by ingesting spores from contaminated pasture | Severe, notifiable, **zoonotic** (transmissible to humans) — do not handle/open a carcass suspected of anthrax; report to DVS/police immediately |
| **Lumpy Skin Disease (LSD)** | Fever, firm skin nodules (head, neck, udder, scrotum, vulva, perineum) appearing within ~48 hours of fever onset, enlarged lymph nodes, skin oedema, lacrimation, hypersalivation, reduced fertility | Notifiable (WOAH); historically caused ~20% mortality in affected Zimbabwean herds during past epizootics; vaccines available (e.g. Neethling strain) |
| **Blackleg (Blackquarter)** | Sudden lameness, swollen/gassy muscle swelling (usually in a limb or shoulder), high fever, depression, sudden death; soil-borne, more common in rainy season | Severe, often rapidly fatal; preventable by annual vaccination before the rainy season |
| **Bovine Respiratory Disease (BRD) complex** | Cough, nasal/ocular discharge, fever, laboured breathing, droopy ears, reduced appetite, dullness; triggered by a mix of stress (weaning, transport, weather) + viral + bacterial infection | Common and economically damaging but generally manageable with prompt veterinary treatment (antimicrobials, supportive care); not classically a notifiable disease but should prompt a vet call if severe or spreading through a herd |
| **Tick-borne diseases (other)**: Anaplasmosis (gallsickness), Babesiosis (redwater), Ehrlichiosis (heartwater) | Fever, anaemia, jaundice (redwater), depression, reduced appetite | Common in Zimbabwe; managed primarily through dipping/tick control; DVS notes these as the other major tick-borne diseases alongside January Disease |

## 3. Basic diagnosis guidance — when to call a vet vs. routine care

**Call a vet immediately if you see:**
- Sudden death of an animal, especially with bleeding from the nose/mouth/anus (possible anthrax — do not open the carcass).
- Blisters/sores on the mouth, feet, or teats (possible FMD).
- Skin nodules with fever (possible LSD).
- Swollen lymph nodes + cloudy eyes + laboured breathing (possible January Disease).
- Sudden severe lameness with hot, swollen, gas-crackling muscle (possible blackleg).
- Any suspected notifiable disease — this also triggers your legal reporting duty (see [animal-health-act.md](../laws/animal-health-act.md)).

**Routine care / monitor, but escalate if it worsens:**
- Mild cough or occasional nasal discharge without fever (watch for BRD developing, especially after transport/weaning/weather stress).
- Minor cuts, general dullness for under a day, mild appetite dip in hot weather.
- Routine dipping-related tick presence without illness signs.

## 4. Vaccination & dipping schedule pointers (typical for Zimbabwean conditions)

| Item | Typical timing | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Dipping (tick control)** | Weekly in the rainy season; fortnightly in the dry season | Zimbabwe operates a network of thousands of communal dip tanks; DVS recommends a "5-5-4" intensified dipping strategy (dip twice over 5 days, 4-day gap) during outbreaks |
| **FMD vaccination** | Roughly every 6 months (e.g. March and September), matched to regionally circulating serotypes (SAT1/2/3) | Confirm current serotype guidance with DVS before vaccinating |
| **CBPP (Contagious Bovine Pleuropneumonia)** | Annually | Where endemic/at DVS direction |
| **Blackleg** | Annually, before the rainy season | Core preventive vaccine given the rapid, often fatal course of the disease |
| **Anthrax** | Annually | Especially in known anthrax-endemic areas |
| **LSD / Brucellosis** | Recommended where endemic | Confirm current local recommendation with DVS/vet |
| **Acaricide rotation** | Rotate chemical class (e.g. amitraz-based for a season, then pyrethroid/combination) | Helps prevent tick resistance building up against a single acaricide |

**This is general guidance, not a prescription.** Actual vaccination timing, products, and dipping frequency should be confirmed with your local DVS office or a registered veterinarian, as they vary by district, season, and current outbreak status.
