# Sheep — Zimbabwe Compliance & Health Reference

> Not legal or veterinary advice. See the [disclaimer](../README.md). For a real diagnosis, contact a CVSZ-registered vet.

## 1. Legal requirements to own, keep, and sell sheep in Zimbabwe

| Requirement | Why | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Identification/branding** | The historical Brands Act framework covers horses, cattle, sheep, and goats, not cattle alone — though our research found most current documentation and DVS/RFID modernization effort centred on cattle | [livestock-identification-act.md](../laws/livestock-identification-act.md) |
| **Proof of ownership** | Sheep are "stock" under the Stock Theft Prevention Act [Chapter 9:18] — theft and receiving-stolen-stock offences apply the same as for cattle | [stock-theft-act.md](../laws/stock-theft-act.md) |
| **Disease reporting** for suspected notifiable disease (especially PPR, Sheep Pox) | Legal duty on the owner under the Animal Health Act framework | [animal-health-act.md](../laws/animal-health-act.md) |
| **DVS movement permit / veterinary clearance** for transport, especially across district lines or during an outbreak | Same movement-control logic as cattle | [livestock-movement-permits.md](../laws/livestock-movement-permits.md) |
| **Police clearance** for sale, where locally practised | Confirms the animal isn't stolen | [stock-theft-act.md](../laws/stock-theft-act.md) |

**Confirm with DVS or your local civil registry office whether an active brand-certificate process for sheep is currently enforced in your district** — our research confirmed the legal coverage in principle (Brands Act) but found much thinner practical/administrative detail for sheep than for cattle.

## 2. Common diseases

| Disease | Key symptoms | Severity / notifiable status |
|---|---|---|
| **Peste des Petits Ruminants (PPR)** — "sheep and goat plague" | High fever, depression, eye and nose discharge, mouth erosions/ulcers preventing eating, severe diarrhoea, pneumonia/laboured breathing | Acute, highly contagious, high morbidity and mortality; WOAH-notifiable transboundary disease; widespread across Africa, Asia, Middle East |
| **Sheep Pox** | Fever, generalized skin papules/nodules, occasional vesicles, internal lesions (particularly lungs), can be fatal especially in young animals | WOAH Class A-notifiable viral disease; endemic in parts of Africa above the Equator, Middle East, Asia — report suspected cases to DVS |
| **Foot and Mouth Disease (FMD)** | Blisters/vesicles on mouth, feet, teats; excessive salivation; lameness — generally milder presentation in sheep than cattle | Notifiable transboundary disease; see [cattle.md](cattle.md) for fuller detail, as sheep/goats can also be infected and act as a "silent" reservoir with mild symptoms |

## 3. Basic diagnosis guidance — when to call a vet vs. routine care

**Call a vet immediately (and expect a mandatory disease-reporting obligation) if you see:**
- High fever with heavy eye/nose discharge and mouth sores preventing normal feeding (possible PPR).
- Multiple skin nodules/papules with fever, especially spreading across several animals (possible Sheep Pox).
- Sudden diarrhoea outbreak combined with respiratory distress across the flock (possible PPR).
- Any cluster of sudden deaths or fast-spreading illness in the flock.

**Routine care / monitor, but escalate if it worsens:**
- Isolated mild lameness or a single animal off its feed with no fever and no spread to others.
- Normal seasonal coat/skin changes without nodules, fever, or discharge.

## 4. Vaccination schedule pointers (typical for Zimbabwean conditions)

| Vaccine | Typical timing | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **PPR** | Vaccinate around 4 months of age; immunity typically valid for about 3 years | Described in sources as the leading small-ruminant disease threat in the region — a core vaccine, not optional |
| **Sheep/Goat Pox** | Core vaccine, per local DVS/vet schedule | Confirm current timing with DVS, as outbreak-driven campaigns may adjust scheduling |
| **Clostridial diseases (CDT — enterotoxaemia, tetanus, etc.)** | Core vaccine, typically timed around lambing, weaning, and breeding | Standard small-ruminant preventive vaccine |

**This is general guidance, not a prescription.** Confirm current products, timing, and any active DVS vaccination campaign with your local vet or DVS office.
